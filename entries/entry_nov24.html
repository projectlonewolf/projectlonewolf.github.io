Description

Today we watched a docu-series named "Unnatural Selection" about the implications and impact of genetic engineering.

While watching the first episode, I annotated who was talking about what, and their insights and perspectives on the topic of genetic engineering. One of the people I found the most interesting was the scientist who actually invented CRISPR, Jennifer Doudna, which is actually is a pretty big coincidnece as her last name ends with "DNA"!

A question I still have though, is why is it so important that it's regulated? Why can't scientists just do what they want? I get the fact that they have to follow laws and regulations, but what could the negative implications of curing a deadly disease such as HIV be?
