Description

Today we did research on the regulations of germline editing in countries all around the world. I wrote summaries and descriptions of them, and also documented what I learned from it!


Exerpt from the document:

It is my belief that the term “designer babies” is unreasonable, but the idea is a good start. According to research and backing of several ethics board, germline editing should be allowed only to edit genetic conditions or heritable diseases, as any other uses could be hijacked and lead to unethical changes to physical characteristics such as changing eye color, hair color, height, strength, and so on. Although the affected people are not born yet, they cannot consent to this, thus it needs to be researched further and studies more, over decades to see if there are any effects to people born out of edited germ lines.
