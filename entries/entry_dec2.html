Today we finished the first episode a docu-series named "Unnatural Selection" about the implications and impact of genetic engineering.

Entrance Ticket | Recall Activity

Scientists have been able to develop a new method to engineer and modify gentic code, allowing them to cure diseases, improve health, change both the somatic and gamete cells of an organism, or even humans.

CRSIPR, developed by Jennifer Doudna and her team, has been able to help efficiently edit and modify genomes thus allowing for any change to be made almost instantaneously. The protein Cas-9 was originally discovered in a bacterium, and has been used ever since. Although there are alternatives, CRISPR Cas-9 is the most efficient and effective

Although there are both ethical and legal implications to genetic engineering, the field has progressed so much and so quickly that laws haven't caught up with its technology and progress, thus allowing for it to be used.

One example of its use is a dog breeder that engineers their dogs' genes to make them more intelligent and more agressive, or better yet, whatever he wants. Another example is a geneticist who is selling CRISPR DIY kits to anyone online, allowing people to make their own genetic modifications such as the dog breeder. This geneticist, Isaiah Zayner was also the first person to inject themselves with CRISPR, which genetically modified the injected/affected cells to grow more muscle.
