GM Crops (main document) Basics - Video

GM Crops can have higher yields than normal crops, and can also have more nutritional value. Usually, cotton, soy beans, maize, and canola are the most commonly genetically modified crops.

Usually, pesticides have been used to prevent crops from being eaten by other organisms. There have been genetic modification for insecticides and weed- killer preventer.

An example of this is golden rice, which have been genetically modified to provide more vitamin-A which most have defficiencies of. There has been reasearch to investigate health and enviromental risks.

GM's Good or Bad? - Video

Although medical uses are usually accepted, uses in nutrition have had controversy. Humans have been artificially selecting organisms throughout history, but people have been queasy about CRISPR-- which forces these genetic traits and isn't completely "natural".

Gene Flow: GM crops and natural crops interbreeding. Solutions include a "terminator seed", which would cause farmers to have to buy new seeds every year. "buffer zones" are also just physical spacing between GM and natural crops. But the question is what's the difference? THERE ISN'T!

What about toxic plants? There have been plants that naturally produce pesticides-- BT crops. That wouldn't be dangerous for humans though, as would only be poisonous to insects. Mainly, the criticism of this technology isn't upon the technology itself, but on the way agriculture is, and the companies that do agriculture.

Positive examples: In Bangladesh, GM eggplants have been able to replace pesticides with BT crops, and it helped the farmer's healths, more money, and more growth. Mainly, we're trying to engineer plants resistant to climate change.
