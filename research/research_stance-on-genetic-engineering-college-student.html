College Student Interview

Quick Summary

I talked to a student who is studying computational biology at Brown University.

I asked him about his stance and experiences with the ethical concerns and implications of genetic engineering and how it should or should not be used in society.

Interview

I talked to Brian Kirz, a college student who is studying computational biology at Brown University. I asked him about what he thinks about how we should or should not go about using genetic engineering in society and how he belives it should be distributed and allowed in the future.

He states that he belives that it should be available and distributed to everyone fairly, meaning that everyone should have access to it, by it either being paid for by the government or by the individual. He additionally said that he belives it's revolutionary and would make way groundbreaking scientific advances in the future.

I also asked him about what he thinks of genetic screening of sperm and eggs to control and prevent any unwanted genetic mutations, and how he views the way it should, or not, be done.
