How CRISPR lets you edit DNA - Andrea M. Henle
Think Empathize Communicate

Notes and Summary

Everything is defined by its genes. Genes are basically the basis of our every trait. Scientists have been working a new technology which allows to edit the genes or organisms and change the traits.

CRISPR is the cheapest, most effective and most efficient way to edit DNA. Surprisingly, it allows for basically any kind of editing to any gene. Another protein called Cas9 is used to actually spot the gene and then chop it off. The rest of the gene is then replaced with the edited version.

Cas9 then has a target RNA which is used to guide protein to actually find the DNA and then edit it. The target RNA is then used to guide the protein to the gene to be edited.

The CRISPR Cas9 complex thus allows for a basically unlimited number of different changes to be made to any gene. Once the DNA is cut, the cells try to repair it, but scientists give the DNA a specific blueprint to guide the repair, it works when naturally it leads to mistakes, so with the CRISPR technology, the DNA is repaired successfully.

Additionally, CRISPR allows to reprogram all types or organisms, not just humans, so it could be used for plants or animals even.

Ultimately, the only limitation to CRISPR is ethics, and the fact that it is a new technology, so it is not yet widely understood.
