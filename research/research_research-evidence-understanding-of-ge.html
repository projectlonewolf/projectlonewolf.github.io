Quick Summary

According to genome.gov, genetic engineering is the process of altering the genetic code of an organism.

Summary

Throughout history, humans have manipulated genes indirectly by controlling breeding and offspring Selection with more disirable traits. This process is called "artificial selection". The process of directly altering organism's genes and genetic code is called "genetic engineering".

Genetic engineering is, as stated by genome.gov, "... the direct manipulation of one of more genes". Additionally, most times this involves a gene from another species being added to an organism's genome, giving it a specific phenotype, generally referred to as a "trait".
